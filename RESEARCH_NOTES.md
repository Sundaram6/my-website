# 11. GROUP THEATRE MOVEMENTS IN INDIA

## Overview

Group Theatre movement in India emerged in the 1930s-1940s as a reaction against the commercial theatre. It emphasized socially relevant themes and experimental techniques.

### Key Features
- Collective ownership and decision-making
- Non-commercial, socially committed theatre
- Influence of European avant-garde movements
- Integration of folk traditions with modern techniques

## Major Group Theatre Movements

### 1. Indian People's Theatre Association (IPTA)
- **Founded**: 1943
- **Focus**: Progressive, left-leaning theatre
- **Contributions**: Integrated folk forms with modern themes

### 2. Theatre Group (Mumbai)
- **Founded**: 1936
- **Key Figures**: Habib Tanvir, Ebrahim Alkazi
- **Contributions**: Experimental theatre, street plays

### 3. Madras Players
- **Founded**: 1954
- **Contributions**: English-language theatre in South India

### 4. Ninasam (Karnataka)
- **Founded**: 1950s
- **Key Figure**: T.P. Kailasam
- **Contributions**: Kannada theatre renaissance

## Puppet Theatre in India

### Tolpava Koothu (Leather Shadow Puppetry)
- **State**: Kerala
- **Meaning**: "The dance of leather puppets"
- **Language**: Tamil (Kamba Ramayana)
- **Duration**: 7-21 nights
- **Puppets**: Made from deerskin, 130+ figures needed
- **Performance Space**: Koothu Madam (permanent theatre in temple courtyards)
- **Audience**: Goddess Bhadrakali (primary audience)
- **Districts**: Palakkad, Thrissur, Malappuram

### Other Puppet Forms
1. **Tholu Bommalata** - Andhra Pradesh (largest puppets, life-sized)
2. **Togalu Gombeyatta** - Karnataka
3. **Ravanachhaya** - Odisha (smallest, opaque puppets)
4. **Kathputli** - Rajasthan (string puppets)
5. **Pavakathakali** - Kerala (glove puppet version of Kathakali)

# 13. HINDI LITERATURE MOVEMENTS (CHHAYAWAD)

## Overview of Chhayawad

**Chhayawad** (Shadowism) refers to the era of mystical-romanticism in Hindi literature, spanning approximately **1918-1936**.

### Four Pillars of Chhayawad
1. **Jaishankar Prasad** (1889-1937)
2. **Suryakant Tripathi 'Nirala'** (1896-1961)
3. **Sumitranandan Pant** (1900-1977)
4. **Mahadevi Varma** (1907-1987)

### Characteristics
- Reaction against didacticism of Dwivedi era
- Blending of classical Indian heritage with Western sensibilities
- Themes: Love as spiritual force, reverence for nature, individual's quest for identity
- Aesthetic and romantic subject matter
- Emotional and personal expression

### Major Works

#### Jaishankar Prasad
- **Kamayani** (1936) - Magnum opus, epic of human psyche
- **Lehar** (1923) - Collection of poems
- **Prem Pathik** (1914) - Narrative poem

#### Suryakant Tripathi 'Nirala'
- **Tulsidas** (1935) - Biography in verse
- **Anamika** (1939) - Notable for Ram Ki Shakti Puja and Saroj Smriti
- **Kukurmutta** - Prose work

#### Sumitranandan Pant
- **Veena** (1927) - Early work
- **Gunjan** (1932) - Dawn
- **Pallav** (1936) - Considered his masterpiece
- **Chidambara** (1951) - Later work

#### Mahadevi Varma
- **Yama** (1939) - Collection of poems
- **Deepshikha** (1942) - Torch
- **Neerja** (1933) - Lotus
- **Rashmi** (1932) - Ray of light

### Historical Context
- Emerged during Indian independence movement
- Influence of Rabindranath Tagore (Nobel Prize 1913)
- Reaction against rigid rules of Dwivedi era
- Search for new poetic language in Khari Boli

# 19. INDIAN ARCHITECTURE

## Major Architectural Styles

### 1. Ancient Indian Architecture

#### Indus Valley Civilization (3300-1300 BCE)
- **Features**: Urban planning, drainage systems
- **Sites**: Mohenjo-daro, Harappa

#### Vedic Period (1500-500 BCE)
- **Features**: Temple architecture beginnings
- **Texts**: Vastu Shastra

### 2. Temple Architecture

#### Nagara Style (North India)
- **Features**: Curvilinear shikhara
- **Examples**: Khajuraho, Konark

#### Dravidian Style (South India)
- **Features**: Pyramidal vimana, gopurams
- **Examples**: Meenakshi Temple, Brihadeshwara

#### Vesara Style (Deccan)
- **Features**: Mix of Nagara and Dravidian
- **Examples**: Hoysala temples

### 3. Indo-Islamic Architecture

#### Delhi Sultanate (1206-1526)
- **Features**: Arches, domes, minarets
- **Examples**: Qutub Minar, Alai Darwaza

#### Mughal Architecture (1526-1857)
- **Features**: Symmetry, gardens, pietra dura
- **Examples**: Taj Mahal, Red Fort, Fatehpur Sikri

#### Provincial Styles
- **Bengal**: Terracotta temples
- **Gujarat**: Stepwells, mosques
- **Deccan**: Golconda Fort

### 4. Colonial Architecture

#### Portuguese
- **Region**: Goa
- **Examples**: Basilica of Bom Jesus

#### British
- **Style**: Neo-Gothic, Indo-Saracenic
- **Examples**: Victoria Terminus, Gateway of India

### 5. Modern Architecture

#### Post-Independence
- **Architects**: Charles Correa, B.V. Doshi
- **Features**: Modernist with Indian elements
- **Examples**: Chandigarh (Le Corbusier)
